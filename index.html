The drying process is used to preserve the shelf life of a variety of agricultural and food products, mainly marine foods. Drying fish in the traditional way is still used In many coastal areas in the Philippines but research shows such a method is found not reliable and cost-effective due to weather conditions and other factors. This study designed and developed An IoT-based direct solar dryer with SMS notification (IDSD-SMS) to address problems on reliability and cost-effectiveness using Arduino Uno and ESP-32. Tamban (Sardinella) fish was used in the experimental study as it is an abundant marine fish product in barangay Calibunan. The IDSD-SMS monitors the temperature, heat index, and humidity together with its temperature range alert condition to properly dry a fish. In addition, the researchers created a web portal as a remote monitoring platform for reliable data visualizations. Results revealed that the System was able to achieve the desired results, the direct solar dryer was able to increase And maintain the needed temperature to speed up the drying time while achieving the recommended relative humidity. The physical and remote monitoring was successful in gathering and displaying the data on the drying process. Moreover, the system was able to send SMS notifications to the user. Therefore, the efficacy of improving traditional sun drying with IoT technology can help alleviate the difficulties and disadvantages that fish drying farmers have encountered. With proper drying monitoring parameters, the study may serve as a benchmark for other food products that can be dried.
